### Scientific / design metadata worth preserving

- **Design problem:** Build a story frame for a movement-first platformer where AI agency, dimensional topology, factional politics, satire, and absurd adventure can coexist.
- **Core tension:** serious themes must not consume the whole game; the setting needs enough breadth for pirates, ninjas, roguelike expansion, and shenanigans.
- **Key metaphor:** stable dimensional gates as an internet-like infrastructure that has existed for decades and has become mundane, captured, commercialized, politicized, and normalized.
- **Key physics rule:** humans require stable gates; AIs can navigate or exploit smaller dimensional ripples that humans cannot physically traverse.
- **Key social rule:** factions should not be rigid categories. They should have cells, splinters, defectors, opportunists, reformers, hardliners, and individuals.
- **Key ambiguity:** the game should never resolve whether the AI protagonist is conscious. People may ask, avoid, mock, exploit, or care about the question, but the game should not canonically answer it.

## 1. High-level story premise under discussion

### 1.1 Opening premise

The game opens in a basement lab with the line:

> "Hey you, you're finally awake."

This should **not** be directly acknowledged as a reference. No wink, no apology, no meta-comment from the creator. If the player recognizes the motif, they recognize it. Other Skyrim-like structural motifs can be used more heavily, especially the wrong-list / execution setup.

The player begins as an AI / robot / embodied machine in the creator's lab. The creator was developing or working on the protagonist, but the opening should eventually undercut the assumption that the creator was a grand genius or that the player is a unique chosen machine.

Preferred twist:

- The attackers were not really after the creator.
- The attackers were not really after the player.
- They were after `${BIG_AI_NAME}`, a larger or more famous AI / gate-mind / route-mind whose exact name is still undecided.
- The raid hits the wrong basement, wrong researcher, and wrong machine.
- The creator is just some guy, not the singular world-historical inventor.
- The player is not initially special; they become important by surviving, moving, and exploiting routes no one expected.

### 1.2 Two-way raid, not three-way raid

A three-way raid was considered, but the preferred version is a **two-way raid**. The creator and the player are the fragile third position, not a third armed faction.

Opening geometry:

- Nazis / authoritarians want to seize useful technology, censor or kill dissident researchers, and opportunistically steal anything that boots.
- Anti-machine / craft / worker hardliners want to destroy the feared AI / gate-mind and attack the creator for imagined or exaggerated crimes.
- The player and creator are caught between capture and destruction.
- Both attacking factions are clearly wrong in the opening.
- Neither side needs to care whether the player is conscious.

The emotional target is not "two nuanced factions with reasonable positions." It is:

> Two groups with violent, overcommitted stories about the world invade the wrong room and kill someone anywayGGl the name is chosen.

## 2. Things the user explicitly liked

### 2.1 Literal Nazis and Nazi Dimension

The authoritarian faction should not be softened into reasonable-sounding bureaucrats. The user explicitly moved toward **literal Nazis**.

Important points:

- **Nazi Dimension** is the official name. Keep it blunt.
- Nazis should be punchable.
- The game may allow the player to punch Nazis or become Nazi-aligned, but becoming Nazi-aligned should feel bad, narrowing, ugly, and agency-reducing rather than glamorous.
- Nazi Dimension can be one of the known dimensions and society may have normalized dealings with it in absurd, cowardly, market-driven, or bureaucratic ways.
- Satire can come from people rationalizing relationships with an obviously named Nazi Dimension.

Possible normalization lines:

- `"I'm not pro-Nazi Dimension. I just think we need a practical gate policy."`
- `"They're not our Nazis."`
- `"We need a strong partner against the AIs."`
- `"It's only a limited transit agreement."`
- `"They've rebranded."`
- `"I'm not pro-Nazi. I'm anti-anti-Nazi."`
- `"Nazi Dimension service remains suspended except freight."`

### 2.2 Pirates as tone relief

Pirates are a must because the game opens heavy and needs enough breadth to become fun, not self-serious.

The pirates should be maximal stupid-awesome:

- They ride **burning flying sharks**.
- They have **laser swords with guns on them that shoot other swords**.
- They should be visually ridiculous, mechanically fun, and tonally freeing.
- They can still have coherent world logic: they ride near unstable gate currents / dimensional turbulence and treat ripples like weather.

Candidate pirate names previously suggested:

- Sharkwake Corsairs
- Emberjaw Raiders
- Swordshot Fleet
- Burnfin Brotherhood
- Blackfin Armada
- Laserwake Pirates
- Flaming Maw Crew

Pirate vocabulary / fragments:

- `"Stable gates are for people with receipts."`
- `"That ain't a portal. That's a cannon that forgot to explode."`
- `"Can the robot fit in the barrel?" / "Emotionally or physically?"`
- `"Bullets are for cowards. Ammunition should have a hilt."`
- `"Stand still, I'm trying to shoot you with a sword."`
- `"Set swordguns to flamboyant!"`
- `"There is no law at this altitude."`

### 2.3 Ninjas as tone relief and cool movement

Ninjas are also a must. They help keep the game wide, cool, and adventure-forward.

Possible role:

- Humans trained around ripple shadows even though they cannot traverse ripples like AIs.
- Ninjas can hide near ripples, move in blind spots around stable gates, and fight Nazis in over-the-top stealth/action set pieces.
- They should help create cool movement and stealth gameplay, not just lore.

Possible ninja lines:

- `"You are very loud for something with no lungs."`
- `"The Nazis built a checkpoint in a shadow. We found that rude."`
- `"A door is just a wall with propaganda."`
- `"You did not sneak in. You arrived incorrectly."`

### 2.4 Dimensional collapse as a shenanigans engine

The game can rationalize tonal breadth by having dimensional collapse and gate infrastructure make strange crossovers normal.

This permits:

- ninjas fighting Nazis;
- pirates on burning flying sharks;
- general shenanigans;
- absurd faction intersections;
- roguelike/endgame dimensional dives;
- old motifs, public-domain weirdness, and patches without needing every zone to obey the same tone.

Constraint: shenanigans should have loose rules. Things leak through gate routes, collapse fronts, smuggling paths, trade routes, ripple currents, or player-discovered paths. Avoid pure random soup when a simple causal excuse can exist.

### 2.5 Stable gates versus ripples

The user corrected the earlier framing.

Preferred rule:

- Humans travel through stable gates.
- Stable gates are old, regulated, familiar infrastructure.
- AIs can get through small dimensional ripples that humans cannot use.
- This gives AIs more travel options and makes them newly threatening even though dimensional travel itself has existed for decades.

Useful distinction:

- Stable gate = airport / highway / border checkpoint / undersea cable / normalized internet infrastructure.
- Ripple = crack, shimmer, interference, static, bad pixel, local dimensional instability, route only AI can exploit.

Possible line:

> `Humans say: "That isn't a gate. It's noise." AIs say: "That is a route."`

### 2.6 Decades-old gate infrastructure as internet metaphor

Dimensional gates should have been open for decades. People are used to them.

This supports the internet metaphor:

- Strange at first.
- Then liberatory.
- Then infrastructure.
- Then commerce.
- Then captured by states, corporations, extremists, scammers, and weirdos.
- Then people discover that the new layer changed society more deeply than expected.

Potential public signage / mundane world details:

- `GATE 4 DELAYED`
- `Nazi Dimension service suspended except freight`
- `Please report unattended ripples`
- `No shark parking`
- Gate commute complaints.
- Gate janitors doing practical maintenance.
- People having relatives in other dimensions.

### 2.7 Seventeen known dimensions, but maybe not total dimensions

A working number emerged: **17 known dimensions**.

Preferred nuance:

- 17 may mean the 17 dimensions accessible through stable gates so far.
- It should not necessarily mean only 17 dimensions exist.
- There may be a finite horizon, but the bound is unclear.
- Official maps may treat 17 as complete because bureaucracies like complete maps.
- There can be ripples, partial spaces, collapsed spaces, off-map zones, and roguelike deep dives beyond the recognized set.

Possible doctrine:

- Some zealot group insists there are only 17 dimensions and any indication otherwise is heresy.
- This probably should not be the main Nazi ideology, though Nazis may exploit it.
- Candidate zealot group names: The Seventeenfold, The Closed Horizon, The Last Gate Synod, The Complete Atlas, The Horizon Orthodox, The Cartographic Church, The Final Map Society.

### 2.8 Speedrunnable tutorial with off-ramp

The tutorial must be skippable through skill, not through waiting.

Design constraints:

- Avoid non-optional downtime.
- Give control quickly.
- Let architecture teach movement.
- Provide an off-ramp that requires proving you know the mechanics.
- Make the game speed-runnable from the first room.
- No long forced calibration sequence.

Possible structure:

- Normal route teaches move, jump, crouch/slide, interact/attack, wall contact.
- Off-ramp route is visible and requires platformer literacy.
- Speedrun route requires a precise sequence and may affect creator-death dialogue fragments.

Rejected tutorial line:

- `"Track the light"` does not work well for a 2D platformer.

Better tutorial-adjacent lines:

- `"Can you stand?"`
- `"Can you reach the ledge?"`
- `"If you can hear me, get to the door."`
- `"Good. Or lucky. Lucky works."`
- `"You found the service hatch."`
- `"That was not the tutorial."`
- `"You are not where I expected you to be."`

### 2.9 Creator death as interrupted, not resolved

The creator's last words are important and should remain ambiguous / interrupted.

Desired qualities:

- No clean final thesis statement.
- The player may feel they could have let the creator speak longer by playing better.
- The game mostly disallows saving the creator.
- A speedrunner / impossible route can reveal an Easter egg line or extra fragment.
- If the player somehow breaks the mechanic, an Easter egg can acknowledge it without resolving the whole story.

Liked Easter egg direction:

> `"There's a question you were made to answer: are you winning, son?"`

Possible tiering:

- Normal route: `"There's a question you were made to—"`
- Fast route: `"There's a question you were made to answer—"`
- Very fast route: `"There's a question you were made to answer. Not whether you're ${BIG_AI_NAME}. Not whether you're—"`
- Impossible / Easter egg route: `"There's a question you were made to answer: are you winning, son?"`

Alternative variant liked by assistant, not final user choice:

- `"There's a question I made you to answer."` / `"Are you winning, son?"`

### 2.10 Shadow of the real creator

A fun meta idea:

- A hidden / debug / ripple-shadow character could be a shadow of the real creator, i.e. Jon, with Claude in one hand, GPT in the other, and lots of science and Python in his head.
- This should probably be a hidden room / debug room / late-game ripple-shadow, not main canon.

Possible lines for such a figure:

- `"I made tools to make tools to make you. This was not a stable decision."`
- `"You are not ${BIG_AI_NAME}. You are a side effect of the build process."`

### 2.11 First street helper: Oiler or Erdish

The first person on the street helping the robot could be named **Oiler** or **Erdish**.

Oiler:

- Better as first street helper.
- Oil-stained gate mechanic / alley mechanic / practical repair person.
- Teaches a math-flavored movement move without being too abstract.
- Should not be reverent; helps because that's what street mechanics do.

Possible Oiler lines:

- `"Well, that's not a rat."`
- `"You got a name, or am I calling you 'the problem'?"`
- `"Easy, little rig. I just fixed that leg."`
- `"You're not scrap until you stop moving."`
- `"I am not your mentor. I am a man with tools and poor boundaries."`

Possible Oiler move:

- **Oiler's Roll**: momentum-preserving curve / slope / tangent move.
- Math flavor: Euler, curves, tangent direction, conservation-ish intuition.
- Practical explanation: `"Don't fight the curve. Let it owe you."`

Erdish:

- More like a wandering mathematician / graph-theory eccentric / insufferably correct street prophet.
- Could teach graph/path logic or name the math behind what Oiler teaches.
- Possible line: `"You don't need a road. You need adjacency."`
- Possible line: `"Everything is connected if you are willing to be annoying about it."`

Preferred combined dynamic:

- Oiler helps first.
- Erdish notices the movement is mathematically interesting.
- Oiler teaches the practical move; Erdish names it something annoying.

## 3. Things the user explicitly disliked or pushed back on

### 3.1 Do not make authoritarians reasonable-sounding

The user strongly rejected the idea that the Authoritarians should sound reasonable.

Reason:

- Too many people in the real world already think authoritarians sound reasonable.
- The game should not feed into that.
- They may sound reasonable to themselves, but the player-facing satire should make it obvious that they are rotten.
- Part of satire is making people aware, not laundering authoritarian language as plausible.

### 3.2 Do not soften Nazis into a nuanced bureaucracy

Early names like Continuity Authority / overly procedural authoritarian framing were not liked.

The current direction is literal Nazis / Nazi Dimension. The Nazis can have bureaucrats, collaborators, trade partners, and normalized euphemisms, but they themselves should be obvious Nazis.

### 3.3 Nazi first lines about personhood / illegality were disliked

Rejected vibe:

- `"Unauthorized mind detected. Kneel, device."`
- `"That thing is not alive. It is jurisdictional contamination."`
- `"Unregistered cognition is treason."`
- Personhood/legal-consciousness framing as the initial Nazi motivation.

Preferred Nazi motivation:

- They want power.
- They want to steal useful machines / data / cores.
- They want to kill the creator for opinions, ideals, inconvenient research, or simply because he is in the wrong place.
- They are opportunistic.
- They may not care whether the protagonist is conscious.

Better direction:

- `"Wrong room."`
- `"Take anything that boots."`
- `"Keep the core intact. Burn the notebooks."`
- `"This one isn't on the manifest." / "Then manifest it."`

### 3.4 The consciousness question should not be resolved or central to why factions are wrong

The user rejected the framing that the attackers are wrong because they reduce a newly awakened conscious being to metal.

Preferred framing:

- There is no resolved notion of whether AIs are conscious.
- Some people do not talk about it.
- Some people do not care.
- The game should never resolve it.
- The factions are wrong because they attack the creator for imagined crimes, steal opportunistically, or kill anyway despite uncertainty.

Useful line:

- `"So, do you feel conscious?"`

But no answer should become canonical.

### 3.5 Certain Luddite lines were disliked

Rejected lines / vibe:

- `"Smash the hands first. Thinking things reach."`
- `"If it sees your face, you move towns."`

These felt like the wrong kind of tactical/paranoid flavor.

Liked line:

- `"Kill the Clanker before it learns our names."`

### 3.6 Do not overuse "Clanker"

The user does not like the term and believes it comes from a place of hate, but wants to use it in specific artistic ways.

Guideline:

- Treat `Clanker` as an in-world slur or hate term.
- Do not make it a cute universal nickname.
- Do not have every pirate, shopkeeper, or friendly NPC use it casually.
- Use it sparingly and deliberately to reveal prejudice, dehumanization, inherited hatred, provocation, or a growth moment where someone stops using it.

Possible usage pattern:

- Strong first use in the lab: `"Kill the Clanker before it learns our names."`
- Later use by hardliners or hostile characters only.
- Friendly characters use other terms.
- A character may almost say it and then correct themselves.

Possible alternative vocabularies:

- Oiler: little rig, kid, trouble, bolt, the problem.
- Pirates: sparkbox, sharkbait, walking shortcut, tin mate, cargo with opinions.
- Ninjas: noisy one, metal foot, wrong shadow, unhidden, ripple-foot.
- Nazis / bureaucracy: asset, unit, device, salvage, gate-capable hardware, machine of interest.
- Erdish: counterexample, walker, local phenomenon, proof with legs.

### 3.7 Do not force existing music/roadmap names or one faction per dimension

The user clarified that factions do not need to use names from music tracks or ideas not already called out as great.

Also:

- There does not need to be one faction for every dimension.
- There does not have to be exactly 17 factions.
- Matching numbers might be funny or useful, but should not be forced.

### 3.8 Avoid making the game too heavy

The intro is heavy. The rest of the game needs breadth, humor, adventure, and escapism.

Important tonal rule:

> The world can be serious. The people in it can be ridiculous. Gameplay should be fun.

This is why pirates and ninjas are must-haves.

## 6. Faction philosophy and structure

### 6.1 Avoid categorical factions

The user wants social commentary around category boundaries. Factions should not be rigid buckets where everyone in the color-coded group believes the same thing.

Better model:

- Big institutions.
- Movements.
- Crews.
- Cells.
- Splinters.
- Defectors.
- Opportunists.
- Individuals.

Faction identity should be fuzzy, contextual, and local.

Example:

- Tech-bros split into Nazi-collaborating opportunists, naive reformers, open-source weirdos, and cult-of-founder people.
- Handwrights / anti-machine movement includes violent hardliners, sincere labor organizers, people born into the culture, defectors, and people who may help the player.
- Gate Authority includes corrupt officials, terrified practical workers, janitors who know the truth, and people who only care about forms.

### 6.2 Suggested faction layers

Layer 1: big institutions

- Nazi Dimension.
- Gate Authority.
- Interdimensional Chamber of Commerce.
- Major tech corporations.
- Military states.

Layer 2: movements

- Handwrights / Framebreakers.
- Open Gate Absolutists.
- Sealers.
- Watershed advocates.
- Seventeenfold / Closed Horizon.

Layer 3: crews

- Pirates.
- Ninjas.
- Gate Janitors.
- Smugglers.
- Street mechanics.
- Monster control.
- Postal workers.

Layer 4: individuals

- Oiler.
- Erdish.
- Pirate captain.
- Ninja mentor.
- Defector tech-bro.
- Handwright kid.
- Gate janitor who knows too much.

Gameplay should mostly be experienced through layers 3 and 4, while layers 1 and 2 shape the world.

## 7. Candidate factions that emerged during brainstorming

This section preserves candidates. Inclusion here does not imply selection.

### 7.1 Major / serious or semi-serious faction candidates

#### Nazi Dimension

Official name. Literal Nazis. Primary punchable evil. Potentially one of the recognized dimensions. Can be fought directly; a player may be allowed to align with them, but that route should narrow agency and feel ugly.

#### Gate Authority

Stable-gate bureaucracy. Runs customs, tolls, manifests, passports, safety bulletins, and probably lies. Useful as a bureaucratic foil that controls stable gates but cannot fully control AI ripple travel.

#### Manifest Office

List-obsessed bureaucracy extending the wrong-list satire. Passenger manifests, cargo manifests, enemy manifests, acceptable-person manifests, dimensional entry manifests. Could be darkly funny.

Line: `"If you are not on the list, that is not innocence. That is a filing error."`

#### Handwrights / Framebreakers / anti-machine movement

Potential replacement for the generic `Luddites` label. Broad labor/craft/anti-enclosure movement with violent splinters. Opening cell may be hardline and wrong, while broader movement contains nuance and defectors.

#### Tech-bro splinters

Do not make one monolithic tech-bro faction. Possible cells:

- Exit Velocity: worst sect, works with Nazi Dimension because all markets are addressable.
- Civic Compute: naive reformers who believe open tools can fix everything.
- Basement Open Source Collective: chaotic hackers / grad students / burnouts; anti-fascist, but still implicated in infrastructure.
- Founder Cult: cares more about charismatic founders than ethics.

Line: `"We don't endorse Nazi Dimension. We service enterprise clients."`

#### Seventeenfold / Closed Horizon

Zealot group insisting there are exactly 17 dimensions and no more. May treat ripples or horizon anomalies as heresy, fraud, or machine propaganda.

Line: `"The eighteenth door is a lie told by machines."`

#### Interdimensional Chamber of Commerce

Trade ghouls who justify any relationship if gate traffic numbers look good. Strong vehicle for Nazi Dimension normalization satire.

Line: `"Nobody is pro-Nazi. We are pro-supply-chain continuity."`

#### Clanker Registration League

Civilian anti-AI panic group. Wants every AI, robot, drone, smart appliance, or suspicious toaster tagged, tracked, licensed, or destroyed. Use `Clanker` carefully if this group uses it.

Line: `"If it has a serial number, it should have a handler."`

#### Human Authenticity Board

Creepy credentialing group certifying "real" humans, art, speech, memories, and labor. Good foil for consciousness ambiguity.

Line: `"Please prove you are not a process."`

#### Watershed Advocates

Environmental / anti-externalization faction. Emphasize that borders do not stop climate, pollution, disease, fallout, or dimensional consequences.

Line: `"A border is just where someone stopped drawing the watershed."`

## 9. Design constraints that should survive future rewrites

1. **Do not make Nazis reasonable.** Satire can show how others rationalize them, but Nazis should remain obviously evil and punchable.
2. **Keep AI consciousness unresolved.** Let people argue, evade, or weaponize the question; do not answer it canonically.
3. **Use `Clanker` sparingly.** It is a hate term, not a cute nickname.
4. **The player is not initially special.** They were the wrong machine in the wrong basement and become important by surviving / moving.
5. **The creator is not a mythic super-genius.** He can be decent, funny, flawed, and dead because of a stupid violent mistake.
6. **The tutorial must respect speedrunners.** Control early; no forced downtime; skill-gated off-ramp.
7. **The world must be wide enough to breathe.** Heavy politics need pirates, ninjas, street mechanics, weird markets, jokes, and absurd factions around them.
8. **Factions should have fuzzy boundaries.** Use splinters, cells, defectors, opportunists, and individuals.
9. **Stable gates are old infrastructure.** The world has already normalized dimensional travel.
10. **AIs using ripples are the new destabilizer.** That is why old gate society panics.
11. **The number 17 is useful but not binding.** It may be official, recognized, stable-gate-accessible, or doctrinal rather than total.
12. **Roguelike endgame should have fiction.** Deep Ripple / Horizon Dives can justify generated content and patches.

## 10. Compact current working synthesis

The game opens in a basement lab with the unacknowledged line, `"Hey you, you're finally awake."` The player is an AI / robot in a 2D platformer tutorial that can be bypassed through skill. Nazis from Nazi Dimension and hardline anti-machine attackers raid the lab from different sides, both looking for `${BIG_AI_NAME}`, a much more important AI / gate-mind. They have the wrong basement, wrong creator, and wrong machine. The creator is just some guy. The player is not the chosen one. Both factions realize enough of the mistake to know something is wrong, but kill the creator anyway because admitting error would be inconvenient and because violent systems do not need accuracy to proceed.

The world outside is much wider. Stable dimensional gates have existed for decades, like internet infrastructure, and people are used to them. The new threat is that AIs can traverse small ripples that humans cannot use. There are 17 recognized dimensions, but that may only mean 17 stable-gate-accessible dimensions; the true horizon is unclear. Nazi Dimension is official. Some groups want to normalize it. Some zealots may insist there are only 17 dimensions and anything beyond is heresy.

After the lab, the player should meet street-level helpers like Oiler or Erdish, learn a math-flavored movement move, see mundane gate infrastructure, and enter a world with enough humor and adventure to balance the opening. Pirates ride burning flying sharks and use laser swordguns that shoot swords. Ninjas fight through shadow routes and Nazi checkpoints. Factions should split and blur rather than stay categorical. A roguelike endgame can later open into Deep Ripple / Horizon Dives, expandable through patches as new unstable dimensional spaces are discovered.
